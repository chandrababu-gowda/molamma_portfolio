Food Chemistry

Food Science is fun. By understanding the principles involved in the preparation of food and through experiments, it is interesting to know more about the food that you eat and drink. There are physical, chemical and biological factors involved in the nutrition, safety and quality aspect of food. Main components of food are the protein, carbohydrates, fats and water. The complexity of food also attribute to the processing and ingredients used for its preparation.

Taste is king. Functional ingredients present in food provide additional benefit to us. Natural foods like fruits, vegetables and leaves provide a 'food matrix' that is more bioavailable to our body. Such foods that naturally contain phytosterols, glucans, antioxidants, good fatty acids, vitamins and minerals provide health benefits. My research also support the fact (as an example), that an intake of 25 gm of soy protein has associated isoflavones present in it and such food matrices are beneficial for good health.

You are what you eat. Each of your cell is made from those nutrients that was extracted from the food that you consumed. So make a healthier choice.

Material Chemistry & Bioengineering

Materials and polymers combined with nanotechnological approaches enable advancement in the field of tissue regeneration and drug delivery. Natural and synthetic polymers have properties that when combined provide hydrophilicity to scaffolds with mechanical strength tailored to match the concerned tissue of repair. Physical, biochemical and biological properties of scaffolds ultimately helped in cell compatibility, proliferation and even differentiation (of stem cells).

Nanofibers are fibers with diameter of nanometer range, and nanofibers of various architectures were prepared by electrospinning. Nerve cells will align if provided with a contact guidance using aligned fibers, while core-shell fibers might be more appropriate for delivery of certain molecules, and random fibers could be used as a skin patch. Micro/Nano particles were generated by electrospraying, and used for drug delivery purposes. Molecules like proteins, growth factors and drugs were incorporated for such application.

Electrospun fibers mimicked the extracellular matrix of cells/tissues and enabled regeneration of tissues, and it helped advance the field of tissue engineering

Analytical Instrumentation

My passion for instruments started with Gas Chromatography when using it to monitor reactions during my R&D work with fine chemicals, such as the preparation of Iso Amyl Acetate, Fructone, Methyl salicylate etc. Research on the quantification of isoflavones opened the path towards the learning of HPLC and LC-MS, while most of the polymer material characterizations helped fulfil my passion towards almost all analytical instruments including the SEM, TEM, UV-Vis, AFM, FTIR, XPS, EDX and other microscopic analysis such as fluorescence/ confocal microscopy for cell-material interaction studies.

Figures and images drawn from plenty of experiments is a fantasy to collect and it raise your imagination, and are impactful when publishing the results of research projects.
