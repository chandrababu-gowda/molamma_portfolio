Food Chemistry

Food Science is fun. By understanding the principles involved in the preparation of food and through experiments, it is interesting to know more about the food that you eat and drink. There are physical, chemical and biological factors involved in the nutrition, safety and quality aspect of food. The main components of food are protein, carbohydrates, fats and water. The complexity of food attribute to the processing and ingredients used for its preparation.

Taste is king. Natural foods like fruits, vegetables and leaves provide a ‘food matrix’ which is more bioavailable to our body. Such foods contain phytosterols, glucans, antioxidants, good fatty acids, vitamins and minerals, and are good for health. An example as part of my learning from soy and isoflavone research is that, an intake of 25 gm of soy protein has associated isoflavones present in it and such food matrices are beneficial for good health.

You are what you eat. Each of your cell is made from those nutrients that was extracted from the food that you consumed. So make a healthier choice.

Material Chemistry & Bioengineering

Materials and polymers combined with nanotechnological approaches enable advancement in the field of tissue regeneration and drug delivery. Natural polymers provide hydrophilicity to scaffolds, while synthetic polymers impart mechanical strength, and were used for the repair of tissues. Physical, biochemical and biological properties of scaffolds ultimately helped in cell compatibility, proliferation and even differentiation (of stem cells). Electrospun fibers mimicked the extracellular matrix of cells/tissues and enabled regeneration of tissues, and it helped advance the field of tissue engineering.

Nanofibers are fibers with diameter of nanometer range, and electrospinning was used for the fabrication of nanofibers of various architectures. Nerve cells will align if provided with a contact guidance using aligned fibers, while core-shell fibers are more appropriate for the delivery of certain molecules, and random fibers could be applied as a skin patch. Micro/nano particles generated by electrospraying were used for drug delivery applications. Molecules like proteins, growth factors and drugs were incorporated within these particles and used as drug delivery vehicles.

Analytical Instrumentation

I have a deep passion towards instrumental analysis. It started with Gas Chromatography while monitoring reactions during my R&D work with fine chemicals, to prepare Iso Amyl Acetate, Fructone, Methyl salicylate etc. Research on the quantification of isoflavones helped me expand this learning to HPLC and LC-MS, while most of the polymer material characterizations provided me explore my learning to almost all analytical instruments including the SEM, TEM, UV-Vis, AFM, FTIR, XPS, EDX and other microscopic analysis such as fluorescence/ confocal microscopy for cell-material interaction studies.

Figures and images drawn from plenty of experiments is a fantasy to collect and it raise your imagination, and are impactful when publishing the results of research projects.
